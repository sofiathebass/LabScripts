Executive Board

Richard Ruan - [Co-President] LinkedIn
is a senior majoring in Bioengineering with concentration in Cell and Tissue Engineering and a minor in Data Science. He's previously done research at USC Stem Cell, UCSF Pulmonary Immunology, UCSF Neurological Surgery, and UCSF Hepatology; and is currently researching Dengue Virus NS1 and SARS-CoV-2 Spike pathogeneses as part of the Harris Lab in the UC Berkeley School of Public Health. After gaining such a diverse set of research experiences involving stem cells in the Bay Area, he has a passion for helping people finding the right opportunity to realize their research passion areas.


Julia Driscoll - [Co-President] LinkedIn
is a senior majoring in Chemical Biology and Nutritional Sciences: Physiology and Metabolism. She came to Berkeley from Connecticut, but is originally from Texas. Julia joined TMSCA because in high school she did research on stem cell applications in disease, specifically skin diseases, which inspired her to pursue a degree in the life sciences. She hopes that by spreading awareness to high school students about this exciting area of research, she can inspire more people to start early pursuits of similar careers. She is aiming to become a pediatrician to help improve the health and lives of children.


Timothy Chang - [Co-President] LinkedIn
is a senior studying Chemistry. He joined TMSCA in his freshman year because he was really interested in getting involved in stem cell research at Berkeley, as well as helping inform the public about the realities of stem cell therapies. As an undergraduate researcher in the Conboy Lab, he's putting the research skills he gained in TMSCA to good use as he investigates stem cell biology and the mechanisms of aging. He is currently pursuing a career in biotechnology or pharmaceutical research so that he can contribute to new discoveries that can greatly benefit public health.
